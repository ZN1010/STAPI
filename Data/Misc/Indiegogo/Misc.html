Cookie Policy

Indiegogo, Inc.

This Cookie Policy is effective as of February 19, 2016.

This Cookie Policy explains Indiegogo, Inc.’s use of cookies on Indiegogo’s Website and should be read along with our Privacy Policy posted at http://www.indiegogo.com/about/privacy. We may amend this Policy at any time and all changes will be made on this Web page. The changes will take effect once they have been posted on the Website. Therefore, you should review this Policy on a regular basis. Capitalized terms, if not defined in this Cookie Policy, are defined in the Terms of Use or the Privacy Policy.

If you do not agree with this Policy, do not use or access (or continue to access) the Website or services available through the Website (the “Service”).

Indiegogo, Inc. (“Indiegogo,” “we,” or “us”) uses cookies and other automated data collection tools when you use the Service.

Tracking Technologies

As is true of most web sites, we gather certain information automatically and store it in log files. This information may include Internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, Internet service provider (ISP), referring/exit pages, operating system, date/time stamp, and/or clickstream data. If you are logged in, we link this automatically collected data to other information we collect about you.

Technologies such as: cookies, beacons, tags and scripts are used by Indiegogo and our marketing partners, affiliates, or analytics and customer service provider. These technologies are used in analyzing trends, administering the site, tracking users’ movements around the site and to gather demographic information about our user base as a whole. We may receive reports based on the use of these technologies by these companies on an individual as well as aggregated basis.

We use cookies to remember users’ settings and authentication. Users can control the use of cookies at the individual browser level. We do not respond to the “Do Not Track” signal that may be activated on your browser. If you reject cookies, you may still use our site, but your ability to use some features or areas of our site may be limited.

We use Local Storage Objects (LSOs) such as HTML5  to store users’ state (e.g. while making a contribution). Third parties with whom we partner to provide certain features on our site or to display advertising based upon your Web browsing activity use LSOs such as HTML 5 and Flash cookies to collect and store information. Various browsers may offer their own management tools for removing HTML5 LSOs. To manage Flash cookies, please click here.

We partner with third parties to either display advertising on our Web site or to manage our advertising on other sites. Our third party partner may use technologies such as cookies to gather information about your activities on this site and other sites in order to provide you advertising based upon your browsing activities and interests. If you wish to not have this information used for the purpose of serving you interest-based ads, you may opt-out by clicking here (or if located in the European Union click here). Please note this does not opt you out of being served ads. You will continue to receive generic ads.
